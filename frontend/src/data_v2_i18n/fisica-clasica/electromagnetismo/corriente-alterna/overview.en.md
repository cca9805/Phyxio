# Alternating current

## General idea of the topic
Alternating current describes circuits where voltage and current vary
periodically in time, introducing phase as the essential variable that
determines how power splits between active and reactive components.

Unlike direct current, frequency modifies the behaviour of every element:
capacitive and inductive reactances control the circuit response and
impedance unifies resistance and reactance in a single phasor formalism.

## What you will truly understand
- Difference between peak value, RMS and phase.
- Effect of capacitive and inductive reactance with frequency.
- Solving RLC circuits by impedance.
- Resonance, selectivity and quality factor.

## Recommended learning route
1. AC quantities.
2. Capacitive and inductive reactance.
3. RLC circuits.
4. Electrical resonance.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Interpret AC as a phenomenon of energy and phase: it is not enough to solve equations; you must justify which quantity is RMS, which component is reactive and in which domain the model operates.

## Connection with real problems
Appears in electrical grids, motors, filters, audio, telecommunications and power conversion.

## Final goal of the topic
Solve alternating-current problems with full traceability of frequency, phase, power and impedance.
