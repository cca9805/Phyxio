# Reactance

## General idea of the topic
Reactance quantifies the opposition that capacitors and inductors present
to alternating current, with a direct dependence on the frequency
of the applied signal.

A capacitor offers lower reactance at high frequencies while an inductor
does the opposite, and this asymmetry is the basis of filtering,
reactive-power compensation and resonant circuit design.

## What you will truly understand
- Why capacitive reactance decreases when frequency increases.
- Why inductive reactance increases when frequency increases.
- How phase and reactive power alter grid current.

## Recommended learning route
1. Capacitive reactance.
2. Inductive reactance.
3. Comparison of both behaviours at a given frequency.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Do not treat capacitive and inductive reactances as fixed resistances: they are phase-dependent quantities that change with frequency.

## Connection with real problems
Power-factor compensation, AC filters, motors and converters depend on this block.

## Final goal of the topic
Calculate and justify reactances with physical criterion to integrate them correctly into AC circuits.
