## Conceptual errors


### Error 1: treating Kirchhoff as a memorized template
Why it seems correct:
Repeated exercise formats reward symbolic repetition.

Detection signal:
Equations are written without explicit node or loop physical mapping.

Contrast mini-example:
When topology changes, the memorized sequence fails despite correct algebra syntax.

Conceptual correction:
State conservation principle first, then write equation.

### Error 2: assuming negative current means wrong result
Why it seems correct:
Negative values are interpreted as invalid outcomes.

Detection signal:
The final sign is manually flipped to force positivity.

Contrast mini-example:
A negative branch current often indicates opposite real direction to assumed reference.

Conceptual correction:
Keep computed value and reinterpret direction physically.

### Error 8: confusing numerical closure with engineering closure

Conceptual correction:

## Quick self-control rule
