## Conceptual errors


### Error 1: Interpreting [[tau]] as full completion [[tiempo]]
Why it looks right:
In informal explanations, students often hear that one [[tiempo]] constant means the circuit is charged.
Detection signal:
The solution assumes no relevant transient remains after one characteristic interval.
Mini-contrast example:
Measured [[tension_del_capacitor]] at one and three characteristic intervals still differs significantly.
Conceptual correction:
[[tau]] is a scale of exponential approach, not exact completion time.

### Error 2: Ignoring initial condition [[tension_inicial]]
Why it looks right:
Many introductory examples start from zero initial state.
Detection signal:
The same charging relation is applied regardless of prior state.
Mini-contrast example:

Conceptual correction:
Always declare initial and final states before selecting equations.

## Quick self-control rule

Before accepting an RC solution, run this short checklist:
1. Initial and final conditions are explicitly declared with [[tension_inicial]] and [[tension_final_de_carga]].
2. [[resistencia]], [[capacitancia]], and [[tiempo]] are in coherent SI units.
3. [[tension_del_capacitor]] trend matches charging or discharging scenario.
4. [[corriente_transitoria]] behavior is transient and physically plausible.
5. Order-of-magnitude check supports the obtained result.

If any item fails, the issue is usually conceptual-modeling quality, not merely arithmetic detail.
