# Maxwell's equations

## General idea of the topic
Maxwell's equations unify electricity, magnetism and induction into four
field laws that describe how charges and currents generate electric and
magnetic fields, and how time-varying fields generate other fields
closing a self-consistent system.

This block organises the integral formulation, the differential formulation
and their physical interpretation, culminating in the derivation of the
electromagnetic wave equation and the prediction of the speed of light.

## What you will truly understand
- How the four equations connect charge, current, electric field and magnetic field in a coherent framework.
- How to move from integral to differential formulation and what each representation gains.
- How Maxwell's displacement current closes the system and predicts electromagnetic waves.
- How to derive the wave equation from the field equations in vacuum.

## Recommended learning route
1. **Integral formulation**: electric Gauss, magnetic Gauss, Faraday and Ampère-Maxwell in integral form.
2. **Differential formulation**: divergence and curl of the fields, vector operators.
3. **Physical interpretation**: meaning of each equation, symmetry and conservation.
4. **Electromagnetic wave equation**: derivation from Maxwell's equations, speed of light and vacuum conditions.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Distinguish field sources (charges, currents) from coupling effects (induction, displacement current). Verify unit and dimensional coherence at every step of the derivation. Do not apply an equation outside its domain (static vs dynamic).

## Connection with real problems
Telecommunications, radio-wave propagation, antenna design, waveguides, optical fibre and every system where electric and magnetic fields interact dynamically.

## Final goal of the topic
Understand and operate with Maxwell's equations as a unified system, derive physical consequences such as the existence of electromagnetic waves and validate results with dimensional and model coherence.
