# Applications

## 1. Residential Air Conditioning (Aerothermal)
Aerothermal energy is the most widespread and relevant application in the current energy transition, where the outdoor air is used as an inexhaustible thermal source. These systems have massively replaced oil and natural gas boilers due to their high average [[coefficient of performance|cop]] and versatility. In a standard home, the outdoor unit absorbs [[ambient heat|q_ambiente]] even at temperatures below zero degrees Celsius, and through the [[electric work|w_elec]] of the compressor, delivers energy for heating in winter, cooling in summer, and domestic hot water production throughout the year.

Physically, aerothermal energy demonstrates that the air, even if we perceive it as cold, contains an immense amount of dispersed thermal energy. The [[saving|ahorro]] compared to conventional electric resistance systems allows the initial investment to be amortized in a few years, drastically reducing monthly operating [[cost|coste]] and contributing to the electrification of demand. Furthermore, by integrating these systems with photovoltaic solar panels, the [[grid consumption|e_elec]] can be reduced to practically zero during sunny hours, maximizing the home's energy independence.

Dominant variable: seasonal [[cop]] (SCOP).
Validity limit: Performance is penalized by extreme outdoor temperatures below -15 degrees Celsius, where ice formation on the evaporator forces defrost cycles to be carried out.

## 2. Heated Swimming Pools
Because pools require a relatively low water temperature, typically about 28 degrees Celsius, the thermal jump with the outdoor air is small compared to heating by radiators. This allows the heat pump to work in its maximum thermodynamic efficiency zone, reaching operational [[cop]] values greater than 5 or 6 in spring and autumn. In this specific application, the [[heat extracted from the air|q_ambiente]] is almost five times higher than the billed [[electric consumption|w_elec]], making it the most sustainable and profitable pool heating method on the market.

The system works by circulating pool water directly through a corrosion-resistant titanium heat exchanger, where it receives heat from the refrigerant. By maintaining a constant flow, the need to burn fossil fuels is avoided, eliminating direct CO2 emissions in the user's garden. It is an ideal application to extend the swimming season without incurring prohibitive energy bills, demonstrating the power of phase change physics applied to comfort.

Dominant variable: [[q_ambiente]] (captured ambient heat).
Validity limit: The system is only effective when the air temperature is above 10 degrees Celsius to avoid the water cooling during the evaporator cleaning processes.

## 3. Heat Pump Clothes Dryers
Unlike conventional exhaust or simple condensation dryers that expel hot and humid air to the outside, losing all the energy invested, heat pump dryers use a sophisticated closed thermal recycling circuit. The system uses the evaporator to cool the moist air coming from the drum, causing the water to condense and collect in a tank. Immediately after, that same dry air is passed through the condenser of the circuit, where it is reheated taking advantage of the energy the refrigerant has just extracted.

This internal reuse cycle avoids the need to constantly generate new heat through resistances, which reduces [[electric energy consumption|e_elec]] by more than 50 percent per drying cycle. Although drying time is usually slightly higher because they operate at more moderate temperatures (about 50 degrees Celsius), this protects the fibers of the fabrics and increases the lifespan of the clothes, representing a perfect exergy optimization of a common appliance.

Dominant variable: [[e_elec]] (electric energy consumed per cycle).
Validity limit: They require more rigorous maintenance of lint filters to ensure that the air flow through the heat exchangers is optimal.

## 4. Domestic Hot Water (Aerothermal Water Heaters)
Aerothermal tanks are compact heat pumps dedicated exclusively to heating water for showers and domestic consumption. By having a large capacity storage tank, they can work continuously at very low power, maximizing the use of [[ambient heat|q_ambiente]] from the room or the outside. This allows generating hot water with an economic [[cost|coste]] three times lower than a traditional resistive electric water heater, which represents a massive impact on a family's [[annual savings|ahorro]].

From a physical point of view, these devices are usually installed in garages or laundry rooms, where they take advantage of the residual heat from other appliances or warm ambient air. By cooling the room air while heating the water, they also act as natural dehumidifiers, improving air quality in humid areas. The efficiency of these devices remains high even in winter, as the cold water from the network only needs to be raised to about 55 degrees Celsius, a thermal jump that compression technology handles with great ease.

Dominant variable: [[cost|coste]] per liter of hot water produced.
Validity limit: They require a minimum volume of air in the room (about 20 cubic meters) so as not to excessively cool the room where they are installed.

## 5. Low Enthalpy Geothermal Energy
In this advanced application, the thermal source is not the atmospheric air, but the subsoil. From a few meters deep, the Earth's crust maintains a constant and stable temperature of about 15 degrees Celsius throughout the year, regardless of whether there is a snow blizzard or a heat wave on the surface. By avoiding extreme thermal fluctuations in the air, the [[cop]] of a geothermal system is extremely stable and high (often greater than 4.5), allowing massive energy [[saving|ahorro]] even in the coldest high mountain climates or arctic zones.

The installation consists of a loop of buried pipes through which water with glycol circulates. In winter, the ground yields heat to the fluid; in summer, the process is reversed and the heat from the house is dissipated into the cool earth. Although the initial installation [[cost|coste]] is high due to the need for deep drilling or large trenches, it is the most efficient and durable climate control solution that exists, as the terrestrial thermal source is the most reliable in nature.

Dominant variable: annual stable [[cop]].
Validity limit: Its implementation is conditioned by the geology of the terrain and the availability of space for the collection field.
