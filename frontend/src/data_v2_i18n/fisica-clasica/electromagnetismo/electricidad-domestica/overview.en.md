# Household electricity

## General idea of the topic
Household electricity is the practical bridge between the fundamental laws
of electromagnetism and everyday living spaces. It covers the physical
principles governing the safe distribution of electrical energy within
the home, connecting power sources with end receptors via structured wiring.

Safety mechanisms designed to prevent thermal and biological hazards are
analysed rigorously, integrating consumption-load behaviour with the
physical limits and economic consequences of utility billing.

## What you will truly understand
- How current flows from the external main connection and how it is distributed and sized in various internal household circuits.
- The fundamental difference between the protective roles of a thermal-magnetic circuit breaker (overload and short-circuit protection) and a residual current device (ground leakage protection).
- The physical origin of Joule heating in imperfect contact surfaces and how fire risks are calculated in degraded power sockets.
- The exact translation of electrical consumption quantities into monthly economic costs, identifying the user's optimal control variables.
- The thermodynamic balance that allows the comparison of seasonal efficiency in domestic heating and cooling systems.

## Recommended learning route
1. **Grid, voltage, and power**: Fundamentals of single-phase installations, root-mean-square voltages, and terminal consumption currents.
2. **Plugs and switches**: Real contact analysis, conduction resistance, and localized heat dissipation.
3. **Protections and safety**: Tripping dynamics of circuit breakers and residual current devices under insulation failures.
4. **Risks and climate control**: The biological factor of the human body under discharges, safety thresholds, and efficiency metrics in climate control.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
You will learn to evaluate any electrical installation or consumption not from mere commercial nomenclature or intuition, but through the application of conservation laws and thermal balances. You will be able to determine the exact current passing through a conductor or a body, associate temperature rises with Joule dissipation, and calculate seasonal efficiencies based on the first and second laws of thermodynamics.

## Connection with real problems
- Diagnosing and preventing frequent trips in the consumer unit due to overloads and short circuits.
- Assessing the real danger of electrocution in direct and indirect contact scenarios and validating grounding systems.
- Optimizing electricity bills by analytically managing contracted power, energy consumption, and time-of-use tariffs.
- Making rigorous technical decisions for the renovation and replacement of inefficient domestic climate control systems.

## Final goal of the topic
To empower the student to make safe, operational, and economically efficient decisions in their real-world environment using quantitative physical models, moving beyond isolated empirical intuition.
