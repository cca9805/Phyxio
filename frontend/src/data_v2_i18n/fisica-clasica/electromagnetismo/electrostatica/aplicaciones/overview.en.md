# Electrostatics applications

## General idea of the topic
This block connects electrostatics with engineering design decisions in
energy storage, electrical insulation and the selection of dielectric
materials for real devices.

It covers how to size capacitors from geometry and material properties,
and how the dielectric modifies capacitance, maximum admissible field
and energy stored in the system.

## What you will truly understand
- How to size capacitance and energy in capacitors.
- How the dielectric modifies field and real capacitance.

## Recommended learning route
1. Capacitors (geometry, C, Q, U).
2. Dielectrics (kappa, permittivity, field limit).

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Do not separate component calculation from material limits: both are part of the same engineering model.

## Connection with real problems
Power supplies, microelectronics, sensors and electrical safety depend on these decisions.

## Final goal of the topic
Move from electrostatic formulas to defensible technical sizing.
