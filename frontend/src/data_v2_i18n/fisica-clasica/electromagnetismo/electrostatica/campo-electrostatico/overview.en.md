# Electrostatic field

## General idea of the topic
This block translates charge distributions into the direction, intensity
and spatial structure of the resulting electric field, using both the
graphical representation of field lines and vector calculus.

It progresses from point sources to continuous distributions, identifying
when far-field approximations are applicable and when explicit integration
over the distribution is required.

## What you will truly understand
- Vector reading through field lines.
- Transition from point sources to continuous distributions.
- Conditions for using far-field approximations.

## Recommended learning route
1. Field lines for vector intuition.
2. Field of distributions for calculation with real geometry.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Do not confuse graphical representation with absolute magnitude and always validate the scale of the model.

## Connection with real problems
Electrode design, insulation and electrostatic simulation depend on this foundation.

## Final goal of the topic
Combine geometric intuition and quantitative field calculation in real problems.
