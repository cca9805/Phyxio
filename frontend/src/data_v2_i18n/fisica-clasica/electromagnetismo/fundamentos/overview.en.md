# Fundamentals

## General idea of the topic
This block builds the foundation of classical electromagnetism from three
core concepts: electric charge, the electromagnetic field and force on
charges, organised within a coherent modelling framework.

It establishes how a charge distribution generates fields in space, how
those fields exert forces, and how the superposition principle allows
multiple sources to be combined without losing quantitative rigour.

## What you will truly understand
- How a charge distribution defines fields in space.
- How those fields produce forces and trajectories.
- How to combine multiple sources with superposition without losing rigour.

## Recommended learning route
1. Electric charge and distributions.
2. Electric field and magnetic field.
3. Electric force and Lorentz force.
4. Superposition principle for real scenarios.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Choose the model by system, scale and domain of validity before substituting formulas.

## Connection with real problems
This foundation appears in sensors, insulation, actuators, particle guidance and electrical system design.

## Final goal of the topic
Solve introductory electromagnetism problems with physical traceability, clean algebra and final validation.
