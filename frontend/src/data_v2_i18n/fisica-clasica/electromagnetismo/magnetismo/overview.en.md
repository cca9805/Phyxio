# Magnetism

## General idea of the topic
Magnetism studies how electric currents and magnetic materials generate
magnetic fields, how those fields exert forces on moving charges and
conductors, and how the geometry of the sources determines the spatial
structure of the resulting field.

The block is organised into field sources (Biot-Savart, Ampère) and
magnetic forces (on charges, on conductors, between currents), providing
a complete framework to analyse any classical magnetic interaction
with full vector control.

## What you will truly understand
- How a current generates a magnetic field and how to describe its structure with field lines.
- How to apply Biot-Savart and Ampère to calculate fields from wires, loops and solenoids.
- How a charge or current experiences force in the presence of an external magnetic field.
- How to distinguish the source regime from the interaction regime in a given problem.

## Recommended learning route
1. **Magnetic field sources**: Biot-Savart, Ampère and basic configurations.
2. **Magnetic forces**: force on charges, on conductors and between parallel currents.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Distinguish cause (current as source) from effect (force on charge/current). Verify right-hand rule, unit consistency and vector direction before operating.

## Connection with real problems
Electric motors, loudspeakers, Hall sensors, mass spectrometers and magnetic resonance imaging.

## Final goal of the topic
Solve magnetism problems with full vector control, symmetry validation and model criteria.
