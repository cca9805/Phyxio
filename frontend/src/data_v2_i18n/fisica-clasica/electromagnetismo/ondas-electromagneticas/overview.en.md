# Electromagnetic waves

## General idea of the topic
Electromagnetic waves unify electricity, magnetism and optics under a
single dynamic theoretical framework. They describe how the self-induced
oscillation of electric and magnetic fields propagates through space
and time, even in the absence of a material medium.

Conservation principles are analysed to describe how these waves transport
energy and linear momentum through radiation, and how the properties
of the medium modify speed, amplitude and polarisation when propagating
in real dielectric materials.

## What you will truly understand
- The dynamic orthogonal coupling between the electric field and the magnetic field that enables the self-propagation of the wave.
- The origin of the speed of light in a vacuum as a universal constant determined by the fundamental properties of space.
- The analytical organization of the electromagnetic spectrum as a function of frequency, wavelength, and photonic energy.
- The derivation and application of the Poynting vector to quantify the energy flux density and linear momentum transported.
- The physical and kinematic variations experienced by an electromagnetic wave when transitioning from a vacuum into real material media.

## Recommended learning route
1. **Nature of EM waves**: Fundamentals of transverse electromagnetic coupling, orthogonal amplitudes, and the wave equation.
2. **Electromagnetic spectrum**: Physical classification of radiation bands, technological applications, and material absorption limits.
3. **Energy and momentum**: Power transport, Poynting vector, radiation intensity, and electromagnetic radiation pressure.
4. **Wave speed**: Behavior of waves in dielectrics, refractive index, and conservation of frequency.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
You will learn to compare systems and interpret radiation not as a static phenomenon, but as a continuous dynamic flux of energy governed by conservation laws. You will be able to interconnect quantities such as frequency, wavelength, the intrinsic impedance of the medium, and electric and magnetic field amplitudes, understanding that any change in the properties of the environment immediately redefines the speed and spatial scale of the wave.

## Connection with real problems
- Designing and analyzing modern telecommunication systems, including radio, WiFi, mobile networks, and satellite communications.
- Applying ionizing and non-ionizing radiation in medical diagnostics and therapies, such as X-rays and magnetic resonance imaging.
- Optimizing and guiding high-speed light waves in fiber optic technologies for massive internet data transmission.
- Understanding solar thermal transfer mechanisms by radiation and the impact of radiation pressure in astrophysics and satellite operations.

## Final goal of the topic
To empower the student to model, calculate, and solve electromagnetic radiation and propagation problems, integrating variables of intensity, frequency, impedance, and medium boundaries using consistent, quantitative physical criteria.
