# Physical optics

## General idea of the topic
Physical optics studies how phase, superposition and the wave structure
of light explain phenomena that the ray model cannot describe:
interference, diffraction, polarisation and coherence.

It analyses how amplitude and phase difference between wavefronts
determine observable intensity patterns, and how aperture and medium
properties modify the real propagation.

## What you will truly understand
- How phase governs observable intensity patterns.
- How aperture and polarisation modify propagation and contrast.
- How coherence decides whether an ideal phenomenon is observed in practice.

## Recommended learning route
1. Interference of light.
2. Diffraction of light.
3. Polarisation.
4. Coherence.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
Think in terms of amplitude and phase, not only rays or isolated intensity.

## Connection with real problems
Interferometry, spectrometry, image resolution, displays and photonic communications.

## Final goal of the topic
Model wave phenomena of light with quantitative basis and experimental criterion.
