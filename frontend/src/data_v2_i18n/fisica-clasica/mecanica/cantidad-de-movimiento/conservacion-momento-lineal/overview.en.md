# Conservation of Linear Momentum

## General idea of the topic
This node introduces conservation of linear momentum as the tool that connects basic quantities with interaction problems. You will find the principle that relates initial and final states when internal forces dominate and the external impulse does not appreciably change the total balance.

This is not a node about "using an equality"; it is a node about justifying when that equality makes sense. Its content prepares collisions, explosions, recoil, and separation of bodies, where internal forces may be huge but cancel inside the chosen system.

## What you will truly understand
You will understand that the total momentum of a system may remain constant even though each part changes its velocity substantially. What is conserved is not the individual motion of each body, but the vector sum for the system when the external world supplies no relevant net impulse.

You will also understand that conservation may fail, hold only approximately, or hold only in one component, depending on the direction of external forces and the duration of the phenomenon.

## Recommended learning route
1. **Principle of Conservation of Linear Momentum**: study this subnode completely because it contains the statement, the conditions for application, the cases involving relevant external forces, and the direct physical applications.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
The physical criterion is to compare external impulse with internal exchange: if the former is zero or negligible during the interval studied, total momentum is conserved; if it is not, the balance must include an external impulse term.

## Connection with real problems
- Short collisions where weight or friction may be small compared with contact impulse.
- Explosions and separations where parts push each other internally.
- Recoil of weapons, carts, or systems ejecting mass.
- Low-friction experiments verifying vector conservation.

## Final goal of the topic
The final goal is to decide, before calculating, whether conservation of linear momentum is applicable to the chosen system, a reasonable approximation, or an invalid hypothesis that must be replaced by a balance with external impulse.
