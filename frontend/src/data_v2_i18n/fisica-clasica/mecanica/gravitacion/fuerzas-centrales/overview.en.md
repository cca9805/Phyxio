# Central Forces

## General idea of the topic

This block gathers advanced topics that need a dedicated space so they are not mixed with more basic leaves. Its purpose is to prepare a structural reading of central-force problems: identify the dominant variable, recognize the condition that reduces the problem, and recover the limiting cases that test the model.

## What you will truly understand

You will understand why the effective potential and the radial equation, together with Binet's equation and orbits, are not just additional formulas. They are tools for deciding the regime, the stability, the consistency, and the reduction of the physical model.

## Recommended learning route

Study the leaves in this order: effective potential and radial equation, then Binet's equation and orbits. This order moves from the structural definition to the advanced application.

## Physical criterion you must acquire

You must learn to distinguish between a relation that is valid because of a physical hypothesis and an algebraic manipulation that falls outside the domain of validity.

## Connection with real problems

Central forces appear in design, simulation, stability analysis, orbital mechanics, fluids, and constrained systems. A poorly chosen idealization can change the physical conclusion.

## Final goal of the topic

The final goal is to use this block as a gateway to advanced models, with explicit control of validity and causal interpretation.
