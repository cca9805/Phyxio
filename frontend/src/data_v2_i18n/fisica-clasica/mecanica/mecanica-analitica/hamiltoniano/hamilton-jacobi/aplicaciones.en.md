# Applications

## 1. Optica geometrica

Dominant variable: [[S]].

Validity limit: longitud de onda pequena frente a la escala del medio.

La funcion de accion cumple un papel parecido al frente de fase en optica. Las trayectorias se leen como normales a ese frente. This application permite explicar por que rayos y particulas comparten una estructura matematica cuando se estudian en regimen de alta frecuencia o accion grande.

The professional question is whether the action front predicts trajectories as a surface, not as a list of points. The criterion joins optics and mechanics.

## 2. Problemas orbitales separables

Dominant variable: [[W]].

Validity limit: hamiltoniano autonomo con coordenadas separables.

En potenciales centrales, la accion reducida puede separar la parte radial y angular del movimiento. The application muestra como la energia constante no es solo un dato, sino una herramienta para transformar una trayectoria dificil en ecuaciones de accion mas manejables.

The professional question is whether separation preserves the correct energy and constants. The criterion avoids separating by habit when the coordinate does not allow it.

## 3. Mecanica semiclasica

Dominant variable: [[S]].

Validity limit: accion grande comparada con la escala cuantica.

En aproximaciones semiclasicas, la fase de una onda se conecta con la accion clasica. El leaf prepara esa idea sin abandonar la mecanica clasica. The student entiende que el gradiente de accion no es decorativo: produce el momento y organiza familias de caminos.

The professional question is whether the semiclassical phase has enough scale to behave like classical action. The criterion marks the approximation limit.

## 4. Metodos de caracteristicas

Dominant variable: [[pi]].

Validity limit: solucion suave antes de causticas o multivaluacion.

La ecuacion de Hamilton-Jacobi es una ecuacion diferencial parcial que puede resolverse siguiendo caracteristicas. The application ensena que una PDE de accion contiene dinamica ordinaria escondida. Cuando aparecen causticas, el modelo necesita tratamiento mas fino.

The professional question is whether characteristics remain smooth. The criterion warns when caustics or multivalued behavior require another model.

## 5. Control y planificacion mecanica

Dominant variable: [[E]].

Validity limit: energia efectiva conservada o controlada.

En algunos problemas de control, se busca una funcion de coste que se comporta como accion. El criterio Hamilton-Jacobi permite leer trayectorias optimas como superficies de valor. The application conecta mecanica, optimizacion y toma de decisiones dinamicas.

The professional question is whether the cost function keeps mechanical meaning. The criterion connects optimal trajectory, effective energy, and action.


## Cross-application reading

Across these applications, Hamilton-Jacobi theory acts as a global method for organizing trajectories. Action is not an abstract letter, but a function that encodes momenta, energy, and families of solutions. In optics, orbits, semiclassical mechanics, or control, the underlying question is whether an action surface can replace point-by-point integration. For students, the benefit is understanding that solving does not always mean immediately isolating one trajectory. For teachers, the benefit is requiring a reading of action front, gradient, separation, and reconstruction. When those elements appear, the application connects classical mechanics with modern methods without losing physical interpretation.


## Transfer criterion

Transfer to new problems requires recognizing when it is better to seek a function rather than an immediate trajectory. First, identify the principal or reduced action that can organize the family of solutions. Then check whether the Hamiltonian permits separation, whether energy works as a parameter, and whether action derivatives recover momenta. Finally, interpret the action surface as a reconstruction tool. This criterion works in optics, orbits, semiclassical approximations, and control methods. The advanced application does not end with writing a partial differential equation, but with explaining what dynamical information it encodes.

Advanced use of Hamilton-Jacobi theory must end each application with a decision about dynamical information. In real problems, this separates a formal expression from a global mechanical solution. A student can transfer the criterion to optics, orbits, semiclassical theory, or control by preserving the common chain: action function, gradient, Hamiltonian, separation, and reconstruction. That chain shows why the leaf is advanced: it teaches solution by structure, not only by direct integration.

## Advanced Application Criterion

Hamilton-Jacobi theory is used when the problem asks for a whole family of trajectories rather than one isolated integration. Its strength appears when [[S]] turns the evolution into a geometry of fronts, reduced actions, and separation constants. In optics, celestial mechanics, semiclassical quantum theory, and systems with symmetries, this approach helps identify the natural coordinates before forcing a direct solution. The useful question is not only what value [[qi]] has at a given instant, but which generating function organizes all solutions compatible with the energy and initial data.

This criterion makes the equation a design tool. If separation fails, the model is telling us that the chosen coordinates or constants have not captured the physical structure. If separation succeeds, the motion is compressed into an action whose meaning connects dynamics, geometry, and phase. For that reason, this leaf should not be treated as an isolated alternative method, but as an entry point to advanced propagation methods, semiclassical approximations, and the study of integrable systems.
