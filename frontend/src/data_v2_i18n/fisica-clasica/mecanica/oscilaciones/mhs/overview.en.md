# Simple Harmonic Motion

## General idea of the topic
Simple Harmonic Motion places the work within oscillations and organizes the quantities used to describe, explain, or predict mechanical behaviour.
The aim is not to memorize isolated expressions, but to recognize which physical model represents each situation and what information each variable carries.
The central question is always which system is being analysed, which conditions are accepted, and which law connects the data with the observed evolution.

This node connects definitions, formal relations, graphical interpretation, and criteria for use in graded problems.
Each leaf contributes a specific part of the reasoning: identifying quantities, justifying the model, working with units, and checking the result.
The route avoids moving directly to numerical substitution and gives priority to a physical reading before calculation.

## What you will truly understand
By the end, you should be able to analyze motion about equilibrium through restoring forces, phase, frequency, energy, damping, and resonance. You should also distinguish an initial condition, a constraint, a physical law, and a mathematical consequence of the model.

Real understanding means explaining why a relation may be used, which assumptions it excludes, and how the strategy changes if the system, axes, or interactions are defined differently.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
The essential physical criterion is to recognize the variable displaced from equilibrium, linearize only when justified, and read amplitude, phase, and frequency as independent physical information. This criterion must appear before choosing a formula: first interpret the phenomenon, then select the model, and only then calculate.

## Connection with real problems
- springs, pendulums, and mechanical vibrations.
- resonance in structures and bridges.
- musical instruments and periodic signals.
- dampers and vibration control.

## Final goal of the topic
The final goal is to solve problems in oscillations independently: choose the appropriate model, justify its assumptions, calculate with dimensional coherence, and interpret the result as a verifiable physical statement.
