# Rotation Fundamentals

## General idea of the topic
Rotation Fundamentals places the work within rotation and organizes the quantities used to describe, explain, or predict mechanical behaviour.
The aim is not to memorize isolated expressions, but to recognize which physical model represents each situation and what information each variable carries.
The central question is always which system is being analysed, which conditions are accepted, and which law connects the data with the observed evolution.

This node connects definitions, formal relations, graphical interpretation, and criteria for use in graded problems.
Each leaf contributes a specific part of the reasoning: identifying quantities, justifying the model, working with units, and checking the result.
The route avoids moving directly to numerical substitution and gives priority to a physical reading before calculation.

## What you will truly understand
By the end, you should be able to extend motion, force, and energy ideas to rotating bodies through angular variables, torque, rotational inertia, and angular momentum. You should also distinguish an initial condition, a constraint, a physical law, and a mathematical consequence of the model.

Real understanding means explaining why a relation may be used, which assumptions it excludes, and how the strategy changes if the system, axes, or interactions are defined differently.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
The essential physical criterion is to choose the appropriate rotation axis, separate translation from rotation, interpret moment of inertia as mass distribution, and apply torques with consistent sign. This criterion must appear before choosing a formula: first interpret the phenomenon, then select the model, and only then calculate.

## Connection with real problems
- wheels, pulleys, and gears.
- doors, wrenches, and lever arms.
- gyroscopes and stability.
- rolling without slipping and rotating machinery.

## Final goal of the topic
The final goal is to solve problems in rotation independently: choose the appropriate model, justify its assumptions, calculate with dimensional coherence, and interpret the result as a verifiable physical statement.
