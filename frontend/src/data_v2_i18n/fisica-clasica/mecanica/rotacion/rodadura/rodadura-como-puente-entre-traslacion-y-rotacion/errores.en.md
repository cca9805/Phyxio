## Conceptual errors

### Error 1: Saying rolling is only rotation

**Why it seems correct**  
The wheel visibly spins.

**Why it is incorrect**  
Rolling also requires center-of-mass translation. [[omega]] alone does not determine motion unless [[R]] and contact are known.

**Detection signal**  
The explanation never mentions [[v_cm]] or the center of mass.

**Conceptual correction**  
Read rolling as a coupled motion: center advance plus spin.

**Mini contrast example**  
A wheel can spin in place without rolling forward.

## Model errors

### Error 1: Assuming no slipping from the shape alone

**Why it seems correct**  
The object is round, so it seems natural to call the motion rolling.

**Why it is incorrect**  
No-slip rolling is a contact condition, not a shape property.

**Detection signal**  
Measured [[v_cm]] does not match [[omega]] through [[R]].

**Conceptual correction**  
Check contact before using the rolling condition.

**Mini contrast example**  
A locked tire on ice translates while its angular speed is nearly zero.

## Mathematical errors

### Error 1: Dropping the radius

**Why it seems correct**  
Both quantities describe motion of the same wheel.

**Why it is incorrect**  
Linear and angular velocity have different units. The radius converts angular motion into linear motion.

**Detection signal**  
Units do not reduce to meters per second.

**Conceptual correction**  
Use [[R]] explicitly and keep the sign convention consistent.

**Mini contrast example**  
Two wheels with the same [[omega]] but different radii do not have the same [[v_cm]].

## Interpretation errors

### Error 1: Reading the contact point as a permanent material point

**Why it seems correct**  
The contact point is drawn at the bottom of the wheel.

**Why it is incorrect**  
In ideal rolling, the point at the bottom is instantaneously at rest, but a different material point reaches the bottom later.

**Detection signal**  
The explanation says the same point remains fixed on the ground.

**Conceptual correction**  
Distinguish instantaneous contact from a permanent point of the wheel.

**Mini contrast example**  
A mark on the tire touches the ground once per turn, not continuously.

## Quick self-control rule

Before closing, check center motion, spin, radius, contact, and graph coherence. If any one of those is missing, the bridge between translation and rotation has not been justified.
