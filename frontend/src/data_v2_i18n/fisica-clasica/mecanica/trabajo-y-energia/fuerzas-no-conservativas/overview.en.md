# Non-Conservative Forces

## General idea of the topic
Non-Conservative Forces places the work within work and energy and organizes the quantities used to describe, explain, or predict mechanical behaviour.
The aim is not to memorize isolated expressions, but to recognize which physical model represents each situation and what information each variable carries.
The central question is always which system is being analysed, which conditions are accepted, and which law connects the data with the observed evolution.

This node connects definitions, formal relations, graphical interpretation, and criteria for use in graded problems.
Each leaf contributes a specific part of the reasoning: identifying quantities, justifying the model, working with units, and checking the result.
The route avoids moving directly to numerical substitution and gives priority to a physical reading before calculation.

## What you will truly understand
By the end, you should be able to interpret changes in motion and configuration through work, kinetic energy, potential energy, power, and conservation principles. You should also distinguish an initial condition, a constraint, a physical law, and a mathematical consequence of the model.

Real understanding means explaining why a relation may be used, which assumptions it excludes, and how the strategy changes if the system, axes, or interactions are defined differently.

## Physical criterion you must acquire
The essential physical criterion is to decide whether forces, energies, or both are the right language; identify conservative and dissipative forces, and control the sign of work from force and displacement. This criterion must appear before choosing a formula: first interpret the phenomenon, then select the model, and only then calculate.

## Connection with real problems
- roller coasters, ramps, and braking processes.
- motors, power, and efficiency.
- springs and energy storage.
- collisions with losses and systems with friction.

## Final goal of the topic
The final goal is to solve problems in work and energy independently: choose the appropriate model, justify its assumptions, calculate with dimensional coherence, and interpret the result as a verifiable physical statement.
