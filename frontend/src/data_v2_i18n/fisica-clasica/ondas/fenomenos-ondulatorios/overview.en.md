# Wave Phenomena

## General idea of the topic

This topic studies what happens when a wave interacts with other waves, boundaries, obstacles, or changes of medium. It is where wave behavior differs most clearly from the behavior of isolated particles.

## What you will truly understand

You will understand that superposition, interference, reflection, refraction, and diffraction follow from phase, boundary conditions, and changes in speed. They are not separate facts, but different responses of the same wave nature.

## Recommended learning route

Start with superposition, because it is the common principle. Then study reflection and refraction at boundaries. Finish with interference and diffraction, where phase and wavelength determine visible patterns.

## Physical criterion you must acquire

You should learn to compare phase, path, and boundary conditions before calculating. If two points or two waves are not in the same phase, the physical sum can change completely.

## Connection with real problems

These phenomena appear in optics, acoustics, antennas, instruments, insulation, medical imaging, sensors, water waves, and propagation in nonuniform media.

## Final goal of the topic

The final goal is to predict qualitatively and quantitatively how a wave changes when it meets other waves or an environment that modifies its propagation.
