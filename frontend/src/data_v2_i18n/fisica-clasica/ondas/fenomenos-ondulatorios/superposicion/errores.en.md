## Conceptual errors

### Error 1: Believing waves are destroyed when they cross

**Por qué parece correcto**

The incorrect analogy with collisions between objects makes students think two waves cannot occupy the same region without annihilating or bouncing.

**Por qué es incorrecto**

In a linear medium, waves keep their dynamical identity. What changes during crossing is the local disturbance [[y_resultante]], not the existence of individual waves.

**Señal de detección**

The student says that after cancellation the waves should not reappear separately.

**Corrección conceptual**

Cancellation is local and temporary. Contributions keep propagating if the medium remains linear.

**Mini-ejemplo de contraste**

Two equal opposite pulses on a string may leave one point momentarily at equilibrium. Moments later, each pulse reappears on the other side.

## Model errors

### Error 2: Applying superposition in a nonlinear medium

**Por qué parece correcto**

Overgeneralization comes from seeing superposition in strings, quiet sound, and ordinary light, then assuming it always holds.

**Por qué es incorrecto**

Simple superposition requires proportional response. If the medium changes because of the disturbance, algebraic addition no longer predicts real evolution.

**Señal de detección**

Permanent deformation, new harmonics, shocks, or saturation appear when amplitude increases.

**Corrección conceptual**

Use superposition only in the linear regime. For large amplitudes, switch to an extended model.

**Mini-ejemplo de contraste**

A soft sound adds linearly with another. An acoustic explosion may form a shock wave and no longer obey simple addition.

## Mathematical errors

### Error 3: Adding magnitudes instead of signed disturbances

**Por qué parece correcto**

Confusion of quantities makes every displacement look like a positive distance, as if [[y_1]] and [[y_2]] had no direction.

**Por qué es incorrecto**

Disturbances have sign. A downward disturbance must subtract relative to an upward one.

**Señal de detección**

The result always increases when two waves combine, even when the drawing shows opposite directions.

**Corrección conceptual**

Before adding, set a sign convention and preserve the sign of each contribution.

**Mini-ejemplo de contraste**

One pulse raises 4 mm and another lowers 3 mm at the same point. The visible disturbance is not 7 mm, but 1 mm upward.

## Interpretation errors

### Error 4: Confusing amplitude with intensity

**Por qué parece correcto**

Both increase when waves reinforce, so intuition treats them as equivalent.

**Por qué es incorrecto**

Amplitude [[A_resultante]] describes maximum displacement. Relative intensity [[I_relativa]] is a later energy reading and changes quadratically.

**Señal de detección**

The student says that doubling amplitude only doubles the observable intensity effect.

**Corrección conceptual**

First interpret amplitude, then translate it into energy reading if the model allows it.

**Mini-ejemplo de contraste**

If resultant amplitude doubles, the associated relative reading is multiplied by four on the normalized scale.

## Quick self-control rule

Before closing a superposition problem, check three questions: whether disturbances were evaluated at the same point and instant, whether their signs were preserved, and whether intensity was calculated only after obtaining resultant amplitude or disturbance.

If any answer fails, return to the local drawing of the waves. The drawing must show that [[y_resultante]] comes from adding contributions, not from adding energy labels or absolute values.
