# Concept of a Wave

## Conceptual context

A **wave** is a disturbance that propagates. The essential point is not that a piece of matter travels from one end to another, but that an organized change is transmitted through a medium or through a field. On a string, each point moves locally; the pattern advances.

This leaf fixes the basic vocabulary: disturbance, amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period, and propagation speed. Without this separation, interference, sound, light, transported energy, and motion of the medium are easily confused.

## 🟢 Essential level

Imagine a stretched string. If one end is struck, a pulse appears and travels. The whole string does not move to the other side; each point moves around its equilibrium position and passes the disturbance to neighboring points.

The local quantity that describes that state is [[y]]. If [[y]] is positive, the point is on one side of equilibrium; if it is negative, it is on the other side. The amplitude [[A]] measures the maximum value of that separation. Therefore [[A]] describes oscillation size, not the horizontal distance traveled by the pattern.

The distance between equivalent spatial repetitions is [[lambda]]. The temporal rhythm and the speed of pattern advance are studied later with more precision. At this level the essential separation is simpler: how far the medium is displaced, how the shape repeats, and what actually advances.

> [!TIP]
> Before solving, always ask whether you are looking at local motion of the medium or at advance of the wave pattern.

## 🔴 Structural level

A wave requires a variable that can be disturbed and a mechanism that communicates that disturbance. In mechanical waves, the material medium supplies inertia and restoring forces. In electromagnetic waves, the field propagates without needing an ordinary material medium.

The key structural difference is between **local oscillation** and **propagation**. Local oscillation describes how [[y]] changes at a fixed point. Propagation describes how a phase or shape moves along [[x]]. When these two levels are mixed, one incorrectly says that "matter travels with the wave".

It is also necessary to distinguish a pulse from a periodic wave. A pulse is an isolated disturbance; a periodic wave repeats a pattern. The quantities [[lambda]], [[f]], and [[T]] become especially clear when regular repetition exists. A single pulse can have propagation speed, but not always a well-defined wavelength.

The deeper structure of the model appears when one asks what is preserved during propagation. In the ideal model, the shape keeps its identity while it advances; in real systems it may broaden, attenuate, or deform. That difference is not just graphical: it tells whether a simple wave description is enough or whether dispersion, energy loss, or nonlinear response must be introduced.

> [!WARNING]
> A wave graph is not complete by itself. A frozen spatial curve does not automatically give the period, and a temporal signal at one point does not automatically give the wavelength.

## Deep physical interpretation

The wave concept organizes a powerful idea: a system can transport information and energy without transporting mass as a net flow. A cork on water moves up and down as a wave passes, but it does not travel with the wave to the shore. What advances is the organized disturbance.

This reading prevents a common confusion. If a crest moves to the right, that does not mean that each point of the medium moves to the right. In a transverse wave, the medium may oscillate vertically while propagation is horizontal. In a longitudinal wave, local oscillation is parallel to propagation, but it still does not equal net transport of matter.

## Order of magnitude

On a classroom string, an amplitude may be a few centimeters, a wavelength may be tens of centimeters, and a frequency may be a few hertz. In audible sound, [[f]] can range from tens to thousands of hertz, while pressure amplitude can be very small. In visible light, wavelength is submicrometric and frequency is enormous.

Order of magnitude helps detect errors. If someone obtains a [[lambda]] of kilometers for a one-meter string, units have probably been mixed. If a negative period appears, a formula has been used outside its physical domain.

## Personalized resolution method

First identify what is being observed: local disturbance, spatial repetition, temporal repetition, or propagation. Second assign the quantities: [[y]] for the local state, [[A]] for the maximum, [[lambda]] for distance between equivalent phases, [[f]] for cycles per second, and [[T]] for duration of one cycle.

Third choose the minimum relation. If space and time are connected in a periodic wave, use the propagation relation. If cycles per second are converted into cycle duration, use period and frequency. If an ideal wave must be read at one point and instant, use the harmonic profile.

## Special cases and extended example

In a transverse wave on a string, [[y]] may be vertical and [[x]] horizontal. In a longitudinal sound wave, the disturbance may be pressure or displacement parallel to the direction of advance. In an electromagnetic wave, the disturbance belongs to the field, not to a material medium moving up and down.

Extended example: a wave on a string has [[lambda]] of 0.80 m and [[f]] of 5.0 Hz. The pattern advances 4.0 m each second. That does not mean that one point of the string travels 4.0 m; it means that a crest repeats every 0.20 s and the pattern advances one wavelength per cycle.

## Real student questions

**If a wave transports energy, why does it not transport matter?** Because the medium can transmit forces and local motion without its points permanently changing place.

**Can a wave exist without a medium?** Mechanical waves need a material medium; electromagnetic waves propagate as fields and do not require air, water, or a string.

**Amplitude and wavelength are both distances, so why are they not the same?** [[A]] measures separation from equilibrium; [[lambda]] measures separation between equivalent points of the pattern.

## Final synthesis

A wave is a disturbance that propagates. The key is separating local from global: [[y]] and [[A]] describe the state or size of the disturbance; [[lambda]], [[f]], and [[T]] describe repetition; [[v]] describes pattern advance. With that separation, formulas stop being recipes and become physical readings.
