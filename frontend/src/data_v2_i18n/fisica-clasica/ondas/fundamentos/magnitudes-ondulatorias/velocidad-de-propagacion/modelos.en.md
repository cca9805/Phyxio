## Ideal model

The ideal model treats the wave as a **travelling pattern** that keeps its shape during observation. In that situation, the speed [[v]] is read as pattern advance, not as local motion of each material point of the medium.

The central simplification is that the same phase can be identified at different instants. With that premise, [[lambda]] and [[f]] describe regular repetition, while [[Delta_x]] and [[Delta_t]] describe a direct measure of pattern displacement.

## Hypotheses

- The medium is approximately uniform; if it changes during measurement, [[v]] is no longer a single reading.
- The wave keeps a recognizable shape; if it deforms, tracking one crest may lose meaning.
- [[lambda]] and [[f]] belong to the same wave train; if data are mixed, the calculated speed does not describe a real wave.
- [[Delta_x]] measures horizontal pattern advance; if it measures height, propagation is confused with local oscillation.
- [[Delta_t]] is positive and belongs to the same tracking; otherwise the distance-time ratio loses physical meaning.

## Quantitative validity domain

The model is reasonable when reading error is below 10 % and when the tracked distance covers a clear fraction of the pattern. On a classroom string, order of magnitude values between 1 m/s and 100 m/s may be plausible; for ordinary sound, the order of magnitude is hundreds of m/s.

For video analysis, the observed advance should be larger than several scale marks and the interval should exceed timing uncertainty. If 0.1 s < [[Delta_t]] and the pattern does not change regime, the estimate is usually more stable than an isolated instantaneous reading.

## Model failure signals

A failure signal appears when the speed obtained from [[lambda]] and [[f]] differs strongly from the speed obtained from [[Delta_x]] and [[Delta_t]] without a clear physical cause. Another signal is a speed that strongly contradicts the observed medium.

The model also fails if the profile stops being recognizable, if reflections mix travelling waves, or if a standing wave is interpreted as translation of the whole pattern. In those cases, the graph may still look clear, but it no longer answers the question about simple propagation.

## Extended or alternative model

When to switch to the extended model: if the pulse deforms, if different frequencies travel differently, or if the medium changes during the trip. Then one must distinguish phase speed, group speed, and energy transport.

The alternative model does not remove [[v]], but asks which speed is being measured. The transition to a dispersive model is useful when an internal crest and the pulse envelope do not advance together. Switch to a superposition description when reflections dominate the image.

## Operational comparison

| Model | What it preserves | Strength | Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ideal travelling wave | Recognizable shape and phase | Calculates [[v]] with few data | Fails if the pattern deforms |
| Direct measurement | Tracking of one crest | Uses [[Delta_x]] and [[Delta_t]] in video | Fails if another phase is tracked |
| Extended model | Distinguishes phase, group, or mixture | Describes dispersion and real pulses | Requires more data and judgment |

Operationally, the ideal model is the first one to test. If its two speed readings are not coherent, the comparison indicates whether the problem lies in the data, phase identification, or the need for a richer model.
