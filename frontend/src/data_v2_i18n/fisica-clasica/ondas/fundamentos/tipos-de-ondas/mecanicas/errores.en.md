# Common Errors in Mechanical Waves

## Conceptual errors

The most common error is believing that a mechanical wave transports matter from source to receiver. In many cases, each point of the medium only oscillates around an equilibrium position. What advances is the disturbance pattern, not an identifiable piece of medium. This confusion appears when the graph is read as a trajectory.

Another conceptual error is forgetting that a material medium must exist. Sound does not propagate in vacuum because there are no particles to transmit compressions. A string without tension also does not transmit a stable ideal transverse wave. The medium is not decorative; it is part of the mechanism.

## Model errors

A model error is applying the string-speed relation to every mechanical wave. The formula with [[T]] and [[mu]] requires an ideal tense string. Sound needs compressibility and volumetric density; a water surface wave may need gravity, depth, and surface tension.

It is also wrong to use the linear model when [[A]] is large. If deformation changes tension, geometry, or effective density, speed may no longer be independent of amplitude.

## Mathematical errors

Rearranging the relation among [[v]], [[lambda]], and [[f]] often fails by multiplying when division is required. If [[lambda]] is sought, compare speed with frequency. If [[f]] is sought, compare speed with wavelength. Another error is using centimeters, kilograms per meter, or hertz without careful conversion.

In the ideal string model, many students read [[T]] as period. In this leaf [[T]] is tension. Period has not been declared as a magnitude here, so it must not be mixed with stretching force.

## Interpretation errors

A numerical result can be correct and still be misread. A large [[v]] does not mean that each point of the medium travels rapidly through the whole system. A large [[lambda]] does not mean large amplitude. A small [[A]] does not necessarily imply low speed; in the linear regime speed depends more on the medium than on disturbance height.

A mechanical wave is also sometimes confused with any repeated motion. An isolated pendulum oscillates, but it is not a wave if the disturbance does not propagate through a medium.

## Quick self-control rule

Before closing an exercise, check five questions: there is a material medium, there is a local disturbance, there is restoring coupling, [[lambda]] and [[f]] are positive, and [[v]] describes the pattern. If a string is used, also check that [[T]] and [[mu]] really belong to the statement. If one question fails, correct the model first and only then revise the numbers.
