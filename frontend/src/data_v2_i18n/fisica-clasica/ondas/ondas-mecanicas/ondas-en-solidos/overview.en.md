# Waves in Solids

## General idea of the topic

This topic organizes the contents of waves in solids. Its purpose is to build a structured reading that connects the main concepts, quantities, and analysis methods with the problems that can be solved.

## What you will truly understand

- Relate the concepts of introduction, elastic properties of solids, types of waves in solids, longitudinal waves in bars, transverse shear waves, relationship between elastic moduli, flexural waves in beams, rayleigh surface waves, p and s seismic waves, reflection and transmission in solids, mechanical impedance in solids, dispersion and attenuation in solids, resonance and modes in bars, modes in plates and membranes, and ultrasound and testing applications to their physical context.
- Identify the relevant variables and the assumptions that allow each model to be applied.
- Recognize limiting cases and validity conditions of the approximations used.
- Organize reasoning to move from the statement to the physical interpretation.

## Physical criterion you must acquire

You must acquire the criterion of deciding which model applies in each situation, which quantities are conserved or related, and when a simplification is valid. Physical reading must precede calculation.

## Connection with real problems

These contents appear in design, simulation, data analysis, technology, and everyday problem solving. Interpreting results is as important as obtaining the numerical solution.

## Final goal of the topic

The final goal is to master the topic of waves in solids with physical criterion: state the problem, choose the appropriate model, solve it with traceability, and discuss the validity of the result.
