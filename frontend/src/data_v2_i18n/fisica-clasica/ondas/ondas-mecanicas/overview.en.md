# Mechanical Waves

## General Idea Of The Topic

This topic studies waves that require a material medium to propagate. The medium provides inertia and restoring forces, so its properties determine the speed and possible modes.

## What You Will Truly Understand

You will understand that there is no mechanical wave without something that can deform and recover its state. You will also see why strings, fluids, and solids share common ideas but do not allow the same kinds of motion.

## Recommended Teaching Path

Start with waves on strings, where the model is simplest. Continue with waves in fluids to study compressions and surfaces. Finish with waves in solids, where shear, flexure, guided modes, and impedance appear.

## Physical Criterion To Acquire

You should learn to ask which property of the medium acts as inertia and which acts as the restoring force. That answer determines speed, oscillation direction, and the applicable model.

## Connection With Real Problems

Mechanical waves appear in structural vibrations, sound, ultrasound, water waves, earthquakes, strings, machinery, material diagnosis, and insulation.

## Final Goal Of The Topic

The final goal is to model mechanical propagation according to the medium, justifying which quantities control speed, energy, and vibration modes.
