# Sound

## General idea of the topic

This topic studies sound as a longitudinal mechanical wave associated with pressure and density variations. It connects a measurable physical model with an everyday perceptual experience.

## What you will truly understand

You will understand that sound is not the same thing as auditory sensation. Physics describes pressure, speed, intensity, frequency, and spectrum; perception translates part of that into loudness, pitch, and timbre.

## Recommended learning route

Start with the nature of sound and its speed in different media. Continue with intensity and sound level. Then study the Doppler effect, pitch, and timbre to connect motion, frequency, and perception.

## Physical criterion you must acquire

You should learn to separate physical quantities from perceptual responses. You should also identify whether a question is about propagation, energy, observed frequency, or spectral composition.

## Connection with real problems

Sound appears in hearing, instruments, room acoustics, noise pollution, ultrasound, medical diagnosis, sonar, insulation, and noise measurement.

## Final goal of the topic

The final goal is to interpret sound phenomena with physical quantities, calculate propagation and intensity, and explain how frequency, energy, and spectrum relate to what is heard.
