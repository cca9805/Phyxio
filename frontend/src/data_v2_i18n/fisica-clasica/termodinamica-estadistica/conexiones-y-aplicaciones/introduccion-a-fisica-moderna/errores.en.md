## Conceptual errors


### Error 1: Thinking classical physics is cancelled
**Why it seems correct**
Because quantization explains new phenomena, it may look as if classical physics becomes useless.
**Why it is incorrect**
Classical physics remains a limit when [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] is very small compared with the scale of observation.
**Detection signal**
The solution rejects any continuous model even when [[escala_energia_clasica]] is huge.
**Conceptual correction**
Compare scales before choosing the model.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A thrown ball does not reveal visible energy levels; an atom may.


### Error 2: Confusing a quantum with a material pellet
**Why it seems correct**
The word packet can suggest a tiny classical object.
**Why it is incorrect**
[[energia_cuantizada]] is a discrete amount of exchange, not necessarily a small classical particle.
**Detection signal**
A photon is drawn as a hard sphere with an exact Newtonian path.
**Conceptual correction**
Read the quantum as an exchange unit linked to [[frecuencia]] and [[constante_planck]].
**Mini-example of contrast**
A blue photon is not a blue pellet; it has more energy than a red photon because of frequency.

## Model errors


### Error 3: Applying discrete levels to every system
**Why it seems correct**
Level diagrams are useful and memorable.
**Why it is incorrect**
Only some systems admit simple levels described by [[numero_cuantico]] and [[energia_nivel]].
**Detection signal**
The problem introduces levels without identifying a bound system.
**Conceptual correction**
First decide whether allowed discrete states are part of the model.
**Mini-example of contrast**
An isolated atom has spectral lines; a warm macroscopic body is not described by one quantum number.


### Error 4: Ignoring the classical limit
**Why it seems correct**
When studying modern physics, one may expect every system to show visible quantum behavior.
**Why it is incorrect**
If [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] is very small, discreteness is hidden and the classical model is enough.
**Detection signal**
The answer predicts visible jumps in macroscopic objects without comparing scales.
**Conceptual correction**
Estimate the quantum-classical ratio before selecting language.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A microscopic oscillator may show levels; a large string looks continuous.

## Mathematical errors


### Error 5: Treating frequency as energy
**Why it seems correct**
Photon energy is related to [[frecuencia]].
**Why it is incorrect**
[[frecuencia]] is measured in Hz and needs [[constante_planck]] to become energy.
**Detection signal**
The result is in Hz when energy in J is required.
**Conceptual correction**
Check dimensions and use

{{f:energia_foton}}

only in formal context.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Doubling frequency doubles photon energy, but Hz alone is not J.


### Error 6: Reversing frequency and wavelength
**Why it seems correct**
Both describe the same radiation, so they may seem to grow together.
**Why it is incorrect**
In vacuum, larger [[frecuencia]] means smaller [[longitud_onda]] when [[velocidad_luz]] is fixed.
**Detection signal**
The solution says higher-frequency light has longer wavelength.
**Conceptual correction**
Use the photon-wave relation and check inverse proportionality.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Ultraviolet has high frequency and short wavelength; radio waves have low frequency and long wavelength.

## Interpretation errors


### Error 7: Confusing energy per photon with total beam energy
**Why it seems correct**
A more energetic photon may seem to mean a more energetic beam.
**Why it is incorrect**
Total energy also depends on photon number, not only [[energia_cuantizada]].
**Detection signal**
A lamp and a laser are compared only by frequency.
**Conceptual correction**
Separate individual quantum energy from total number of quanta.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Many red photons can carry more total energy than a few violet photons.


### Error 8: Reading negative energy as impossible
**Why it seems correct**
Some models use a chosen zero of energy.
**Why it is incorrect**
The sign of [[energia_nivel]] depends on reference zero; differences like [[separacion_energia]] are physically decisive.
**Detection signal**
A bound level is rejected just because it is negative.
**Conceptual correction**
Ask what zero of energy is chosen and what energy gap controls the transition.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A bound electron can have negative relative energy and still be physical.

## Quick self-control rule

Identify the scale, check units, compare [[energia_cuantizada]] or [[separacion_energia]] with [[escala_energia_clasica]], and decide whether [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] allows a classical approximation.
