## Historical problem

At the end of the nineteenth century, classical physics seemed able to explain motion, waves, heat, and electromagnetism. Yet several problems resisted it. Blackbody radiation, the photoelectric effect, and atomic spectra did not fit a picture where energy was always continuous. The underlying question was why some systems exchange energy only in specific amounts.

## Prior conceptual difficulty

The difficulty was deep because continuity felt natural. A classical wave could have any amplitude and carry any energy. Experiments, however, suggested thresholds, discrete lines, and dependence on [[frecuencia]], not only intensity. Without [[energia_cuantizada]], the relation between light and matter remained incomplete.

## What changed

Planck's introduction of [[constante_planck]] in 1900 was the first formal break with continuity: blackbody radiation was only explained if energy was emitted in packets. Einstein extended the idea in 1905, proposing that light itself travels as individual photons, each carrying [[energia_cuantizada]] proportional to [[frecuencia]]. Bohr's 1913 atomic model applied quantization to bound electrons: [[numero_cuantico]] labeled each allowed state and [[energia_nivel]] specified its energy. Transitions between levels release exactly [[separacion_energia]], which connects the emitted [[longitud_onda]] to the discrete atomic structure. These three steps turned a mathematical convenience into a physical principle.

## Impact on physics

Modern physics did not erase everything classical; it defined its domain. Classical physics emerges when [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] is small, but fails when [[separacion_energia]] is comparable with the scale of the phenomenon. This scale criterion is the most operationally useful legacy of the quantum revolution.

## Connection with modern physics

Today this idea supports sensors, lasers, spectroscopy, semiconductors, and quantum models of matter. The leaf connects that history with an operational rule: compare scales to decide whether classical continuity is enough.
