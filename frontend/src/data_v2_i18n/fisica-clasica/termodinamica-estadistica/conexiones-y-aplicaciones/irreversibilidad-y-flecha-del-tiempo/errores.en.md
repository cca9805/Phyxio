## Conceptual errors


### Error 1: Confusing entropy with visual disorder
**Why it seems correct**
Messy pictures are easy to remember.
**Why it is incorrect**
[[entropia]] depends on [[numero_microestados]], not on appearance.
**Detection signal**
The sign is decided by looking at visual chaos.
**Conceptual correction**
Connect entropy with compatible microstates and probability.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A crystal may look ordered and still have thermal entropy.


### Error 2: Thinking the time arrow is only psychological
**Why it seems correct**
We remember the past, so it may seem mental.
**Why it is incorrect**
The thermodynamic arrow is expressed by [[variacion_entropia_universo]].
**Detection signal**
No system-surroundings balance appears.
**Conceptual correction**
Use the total second law as the physical criterion.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Ink spreads even if nobody watches it.

## Model errors


### Error 3: Forgetting the surroundings
**Why it seems correct**
The system is usually what is drawn.
**Why it is incorrect**
The second law applies to system plus surroundings.
**Detection signal**
A violation is claimed because [[variacion_entropia_sistema]] decreases.
**Conceptual correction**
Add [[variacion_entropia_entorno]] before interpreting.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A refrigerator cools inside but heats the room more.


### Error 4: Applying equilibrium where it is absent
**Why it seems correct**
[[entropia]] formulas are familiar.
**Why it is incorrect**
Without defined macroscopic states, state [[entropia]] may be ambiguous.
**Detection signal**
A fast complex process is assigned one state without justification.
**Conceptual correction**
State whether local equilibrium is valid or use an extended model.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Free expansion can use a global balance; a complex shock may require transport.

## Mathematical errors


### Error 5: Adding incompatible entropy units
**Why it seems correct**
All terms look like entropy changes.
**Why it is incorrect**
J/K and multiples of [[constante_boltzmann]] require conversion.
**Detection signal**
Microscopic and macroscopic scales are mixed.
**Conceptual correction**
Check units before addition.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Do not add 1 J/K and 3 [[constante_boltzmann]] without conversion.


### Error 6: Using the local sign as the total sign
**Why it seems correct**
The sign of one part seems decisive.
**Why it is incorrect**
[[variacion_entropia_sistema]] is not [[variacion_entropia_universo]].
**Detection signal**
[[variacion_entropia_entorno]] is missing.
**Conceptual correction**
Compute the total before discussing the arrow.
**Mini-example of contrast**
Local crystallization can be compensated by heat to the surroundings.

## Interpretation errors


### Error 7: Reading reversible as a movie running backward
**Why it seems correct**
The word reversible suggests rewinding.
**Why it is incorrect**
Reversible means no [[entropia_producida]], not any visual reversal.
**Detection signal**
A visual path is confused with a thermodynamic limit.
**Conceptual correction**
Read reversibility as ideal zero entropy production.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A quasi-static compression is not just any reversed recording.


### Error 8: Treating a fluctuation as a macroscopic rule
**Why it seems correct**
Fluctuations can reduce entropy locally.
**Why it is incorrect**
For large systems, macroscopic reversal probability is negligible.
**Detection signal**
A microscopic exception is used to deny the second law.
**Conceptual correction**
Distinguish microscopic possibility from macroscopic tendency.
**Mini-example of contrast**
A few molecules may cluster randomly; a whole gas cloud does not separate spontaneously.

## Quick self-control rule

Define system and surroundings, sum the total balance, check units, and decide the arrow of time only with [[variacion_entropia_universo]] and [[entropia_producida]].
