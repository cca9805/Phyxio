## Ideal model

The ideal model describes a dilute gas in equilibrium, made of many identical particles of [[masa_particula]] moving with different [[velocidad]]. The Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution predicts the [[densidad_probabilidad_velocidad]] of those speeds when the only relevant thermal scale is [[temperatura_absoluta]]. It does not track a specific molecule. It represents the statistical population. In the graph, the peak marks [[velocidad_mas_probable]], while areas under the curve give [[probabilidad_intervalo]].

## Hypotheses

- Dilute ideal gas: if interactions are strong, [[densidad_probabilidad_velocidad]] no longer depends only on [[temperatura_absoluta]] and [[masa_particula]].
- Thermal equilibrium: without enough collisions, the gas may show a distorted or multi-peaked distribution.
- Identical particles: in a gas mixture, each species has its own [[masa_particula]] and its own characteristic speeds.
- Classical nonrelativistic regime: if [[velocidad_rms]] becomes an appreciable fraction of light speed, another model is needed.
- Macroscopic population: with too few particles, a continuous curve is only a rough guide.

## Quantitative validity domain

The model is reliable for dilute gases whose mean free path is much larger than molecular size and whose collisions are mainly binary. A practical criterion is that the thermal energy scale set by [[constante_boltzmann]] and [[temperatura_absoluta]] should not trigger dominant internal excitation, dissociation, or ionization. For air near 300 K, characteristic speeds of hundreds of m/s are reasonable. If [[velocidad_rms]] is below about 0.01 c, the nonrelativistic treatment is safe. For interval questions, [[probabilidad_intervalo]] must be obtained from [[velocidad_minima]] and [[velocidad_maxima]], not from a single curve height.

## Model failure signals

An observed curve with two persistent peaks suggests mixed populations, molecular beams, or lack of equilibrium. An excessive fast tail may indicate external acceleration, plasma behavior, chemical reactions, or nonuniform heating. If the computed [[velocidad_media]] does not lie between [[velocidad_mas_probable]] and [[velocidad_rms]], there is a mathematical or unit error. The model also fails if Celsius values are used instead of [[temperatura_absoluta]].

## Extended or alternative model

For dense gases, real-gas corrections are needed. For plasmas or molecular beams, non-Maxwellian or time-dependent kinetic models are more appropriate. At very low [[temperatura_absoluta]] or high density, quantum statistics can matter. For mixtures, one distribution is built for each [[masa_particula]], and the measured signal is interpreted as a combination.


When a higher precision calculation is preferred, switching to the extended or alternative model introduces second-order corrections.
## Operational comparison

| Situation | Simple model | Change model if |
| Dilute ideal gas | Maxwell-Boltzmann | Strong interactions appear |
| Thermal equilibrium | Single smooth curve | Two peaks persist |
| One species | One [[masa_particula]] | A mixture is present |
| Moderate speeds | Classical treatment | [[velocidad_rms]] approaches c |
| Speed interval | Area under curve | Only peak height is used |
