## Ideal model

The ideal model assumes that a phase can be distinguished by a single macroscopic [[parametro_de_orden]]. In the disordered phase this value is zero or averages to zero; in the ordered phase it becomes finite. The [[temperatura]] acts as the control variable, and the [[temperatura_critica]] marks where the organization changes. If the transition is continuous, the [[parametro_de_orden]] approaches zero smoothly. If a [[salto_parametro_de_orden]] appears, the transition is interpreted as abrupt.

## Hypotheses

- A relevant [[parametro_de_orden]] exists. If it is poorly chosen, two different phases may look identical.
- The [[temperatura_critica]] is well defined. If impurities or finite size dominate, the transition may broaden.
- The sign convention of the [[parametro_de_orden]] remains fixed. If not, orientation reversal can be mistaken for new physics.
- The [[campo_conjugado]] is small when [[susceptibilidad]] is used. If it is large, the response is no longer linear.
- The critical law is applied only near the transition. Far from [[temperatura_critica]], [[amplitud_critica]] and [[exponente_critico_beta]] lose diagnostic meaning.

## Quantitative validity domain

A useful critical reading usually needs |[[temperatura_reducida]]| < 10^-1, and refined fits often require |[[temperatura_reducida]]| < 10^-2. A [[salto_parametro_de_orden]] should exceed experimental noise, for instance at least 3 times the measurement uncertainty. Linear response requires the change induced by [[campo_conjugado]] to remain small compared with the characteristic [[parametro_de_orden]]. Estimating [[exponente_critico_beta]] is more reliable when data cover at least one decade of |[[temperatura_reducida]]|.

## Model failure signals

The model fails if the chosen [[parametro_de_orden]] does not distinguish the observed phases, if there is strong hysteresis, if the value depends heavily on thermal history, or if zero does not represent restored symmetry. It also fails when [[susceptibilidad]] depends on perturbation size, because that signals nonlinear response. Close to [[temperatura_critica]], strong fluctuations may require a richer model. That enrichment typically involves treating spatially extended correlations and fluctuation-driven corrections that go beyond the simple mean-field picture.

## Extended or alternative model

If the system needs several components of order, one uses a vector or tensor [[parametro_de_orden]]. If fluctuations dominate, Landau-Ginzburg theory or critical-scaling models are more appropriate. In coexistence transitions, [[salto_parametro_de_orden]] must be combined with free-energy and hysteresis evidence.


When a higher precision calculation is preferred, switching to the extended or alternative model introduces second-order corrections.
