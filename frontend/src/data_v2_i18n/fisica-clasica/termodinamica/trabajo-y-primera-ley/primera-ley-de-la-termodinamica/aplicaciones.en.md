# Applications

## 1. Laboratory calorimetry

In a simple calorimeter, the goal is to relate heat entering or leaving a sample to the change in its internal energy. If the container is rigid and expansion is negligible, [[trabajo_sobre_sistema]] can be treated as zero, so [[calor_intercambiado]] dominates the balance. This makes it possible to measure heat capacities, reaction heats, or energy released during physical processes. The system definition matters: if the system is only the sample, heat exchanged with the calorimeter water crosses the boundary; if the system is sample plus water, the boundary and interpretation change.

Dominant variable: [[calor_intercambiado]]
Validity limit: thermal losses to the surroundings below 5 % of the measured heat.

For teaching, this case separates measurement from model. A thermometer does not directly measure [[variacion_energia_interna]]; it measures temperature changes that are converted into energy using material properties. The teacher can use the experiment to show why a poor system definition changes the sign of [[calor_intercambiado]]. It also introduces experimental uncertainty: imperfect insulation, heat capacity of the container, and delay in reading the thermometer.

## 2. Gas compression in pistons

When a piston compresses a gas, the surroundings do [[trabajo_sobre_sistema]]. If compression is fast, [[calor_intercambiado]] with the outside may be small and the gas increases its [[variacion_energia_interna]]. If compression is slow, part of the mechanical energy can leave as heat while the work is being done. This application predicts whether the gas warms, how much internal energy changes, and what part of the mechanical energy remains temporarily stored. The same reasoning applies to hand pumps, compressors, and engine stages.

Dominant variable: [[trabajo_sobre_sistema]]
Validity limit: closed system with negligible gas leakage during compression.

The functional reading is direct: the force applied to the piston is not lost; it crosses the boundary as work. If the gas cannot reject heat quickly, that input increases [[variacion_energia_interna]]. In classroom problems, this application compares fast and slow compression without changing the physical law. What changes is the value of [[calor_intercambiado]], not the conservation principle.

## 3. Heat engines

In a heat engine, the working fluid receives [[calor_intercambiado]] from a hot source, performs work on the outside, and returns close to its initial state. Over a complete cycle, [[energia_interna_final]] and [[energia_interna_inicial]] coincide, so [[variacion_energia_interna]] must be zero for the full loop. This application shows that the engine does not create energy: it converts part of the heat received into useful work and rejects the rest to a cold source. The first law imposes the balance, although it does not determine maximum efficiency by itself.

Dominant variable: [[variacion_energia_interna]]
Validity limit: closed cycle returning to the same thermodynamic state within experimental uncertainty.

For students, the important point is that an engine can produce net work without accumulating internal energy loop after loop. The working fluid is thermodynamically reset when the cycle closes. For teachers, this application opens the path toward efficiency without introducing the full second law yet: first energy conservation is established, then one asks why not all thermal energy can become useful work.

## 4. Refrigerators and heat pumps

A refrigerator extracts heat from a cold region and rejects heat to a warmer region because work is supplied to the compressor. If the refrigerant is chosen as the system, [[trabajo_sobre_sistema]] enters during compression and [[calor_intercambiado]] changes sign depending on the heat exchanger considered. The first law checks that the heat expelled to the room is larger than the heat extracted from the interior, because it also includes the electrical work supplied. This prevents the misconception that the refrigerator destroys heat.

Dominant variable: [[trabajo_sobre_sistema]]
Validity limit: steady cyclic operation with no net internal energy accumulation.

This application also shows the importance of choosing the system. If the room is the system, the refrigerator adds net heat to it. If the cold interior is the system, one observes extraction of [[calor_intercambiado]]. If the refrigerant is the system, alternating stages of compression, expansion, and heat transfer appear. The law is unchanged, but the balance arrows change with the chosen boundary.

## 5. Geophysics and atmosphere

In atmospheric air parcels, the first law explains why air cools when it expands while rising and warms when compressed while descending. In an adiabatic approximation, [[calor_intercambiado]] is small during rapid vertical motion, so the change in [[variacion_energia_interna]] is associated with expansion or compression work. This application connects energy balance with thermal gradients, cloud formation, and atmospheric stability. Although the real atmosphere exchanges radiation and moisture, the adiabatic case gives the first physical scale for weather interpretation.

Dominant variable: [[variacion_energia_interna]]
Validity limit: motion fast enough to neglect heat exchange and without significant condensation.

The value of this application is that it connects a laboratory law with visible phenomena: rising warm air, slope cooling, cloud formation, and warming by subsidence. When condensation occurs, latent heat release modifies the effective [[calor_intercambiado]] and the dry model is no longer sufficient. The first law is not abandoned; it is enriched with additional physical terms.
