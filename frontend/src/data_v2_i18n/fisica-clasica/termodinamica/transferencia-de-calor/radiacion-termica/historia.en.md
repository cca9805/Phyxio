## Historical problem

Thermal radiation became a central problem when physicists tried to explain how hot bodies emit. It was known that an object changed color and emitted more energy as [[temperatura_absoluta]] increased, but a clear relation between [[potencia_radiada]], [[area_superficie]], and temperature was missing. The issue was not only practical: furnaces, lamps, and stars showed that thermal emission had a deep structure.

## Prior conceptual difficulty

Intuition suggested a smooth, almost linear relation between temperature and emission. Experiments showed something much stronger. Real surfaces also did not emit equally: physicists had to distinguish an ideal blackbody from surfaces with lower [[emisividad]]. The notion of [[flujo_radiativo]] helped separate emission per unit surface from total power.

## What changed

Stefan found experimentally that total emission depended on the fourth power of temperature. Boltzmann later gave a theoretical justification using thermodynamics and electromagnetism. That synthesis connected [[potencia_radiada]], [[constante_stefan_boltzmann]], [[area_superficie]], [[emisividad]], and [[temperatura_absoluta]].

Thermal radiation changed from a qualitative observation about glow into a quantitative law.

## Impact on physics

The impact was double. In engineering it allowed radiative losses, furnaces, sensors, and energy balances to be calculated. In fundamental physics it opened an unexpected door: the study of the blackbody spectrum led Planck to energy quantization. The idea of [[potencia_neta]] also showed that bodies do not merely emit; they exchange radiation with surroundings described by [[temperatura_ambiente]].

## Connection with modern physics

Today thermal radiation appears in climate science, astronomy, satellites, infrared cameras, and nanotechnology. The modern interpretation keeps the historical core: [[temperatura_absoluta]] dominates emission, [[area_superficie]] scales total power, [[emisividad]] corrects the real surface, and [[temperatura_ambiente]] decides the net balance. This leaf begins in heat transfer and ends brushing against quantum physics.
