# Physical applications

## 1. School laboratory

It appears in every measurement made with a ruler, stopwatch, balance, or sensor. It calculates which figures to keep and which uncertainty to report. The result means honest precision. The dangerous error is writing many decimals and pretending to have instrumental exactness. As a final control, the learner must explain which datum changes, which quantity is calculated, and which physical conclusion can accept or reject the result.

## 2. Experimental kinematics

It appears when measuring distances and times to obtain speeds or accelerations. It calculates how each data uncertainty affects the result. The result indicates whether a difference is real or compatible with measurement error. The dangerous error is comparing numbers without uncertainty. ## 3. Dynamics

It appears when calculating forces from measured mass and acceleration. It estimates force uncertainty. The result helps decide whether two forces are distinguishable.
