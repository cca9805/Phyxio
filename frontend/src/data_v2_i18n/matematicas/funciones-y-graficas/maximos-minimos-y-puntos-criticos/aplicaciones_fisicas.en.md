# Physical applications

## 1. Potential energy

It appears when studying equilibrium. Minima and maxima of energy are calculated. The result means stability if the minimum rises on both sides, or instability if the point is a maximum. The dangerous error is calling every zero-slope point stable.

## 2. Projectile motion

It appears when searching for maximum height or optimal range. The extremum of a trajectory or angle-dependent expression is calculated. The result means a maximum condition inside the model. The dangerous error is forgetting air resistance or the domain of allowed angles.

## 3. Resonance

It appears in graphs of amplitude versus frequency. The maximum response is calculated and the resonance frequency is identified inside the driven interval. The result means the frequency where the system absorbs energy most effectively. The dangerous error is confusing maximum amplitude with system stability.

## 4. Thermodynamics and stability

It appears in thermodynamic potentials. Minima compatible with constraints such as fixed temperature, pressure, or volume are calculated. The result means stable state or equilibrium under fixed conditions. The dangerous error is ignoring that the relevant function changes with controlled variables.

## 5. Experimental optimization

It appears when searching for maximum power, minimum loss, or best efficiency. An optimum condition is calculated inside measured data. The result guides design and decisions. The dangerous error is accepting an apparent extremum produced by noise or too few points.
