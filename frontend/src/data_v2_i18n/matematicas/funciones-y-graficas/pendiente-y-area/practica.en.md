# Practice

## Level 1: recognition

Look at three graphs: `x-t`, `v-t`, and `F-t`. For each one, write what calculating slope would mean and what calculating area would mean. Do not calculate yet; only identify the operation and the resulting physical quantity.

Mentally mark regions where the curve rises, falls, is horizontal, lies above the axis, and lies below the axis. Explain what sign the slope would have and what sign the area would have in each region.

## Level 2: direct application

Calculate the average slope of a position-time graph passing through `(1 s, 2 m)` and `(5 s, 14 m)`. Write the quotient, the unit, and a physical sentence beginning with "position changes...".

Calculate the area under a rectangular velocity-time graph with `v = 4 m/s` between `t = 0 s` and `t = 6 s`. Write the result in meters and explain why it is not a velocity.

## Level 3: choosing the tool

Decide whether each question asks for slope, area, or direct reading: "what average velocity did the object have?", "what net distance did it travel?", "what value did the force have at `2 s`?", "what impulse did it receive?". Justify each choice with a keyword from the prompt.

Compare a triangular `v-t` graph with base `8 s` and height `6 m/s` with a rectangular graph of base `4 s` and height `6 m/s`. Calculate both areas and explain which one produces greater accumulated displacement.

## Level 4: physical interpretation

A `v-t` graph has positive area `20 m` and negative area `-8 m`. Calculate net displacement and explain why it does not necessarily match total distance traveled.

An `F-x` graph shows force increasing from `0 N` to `10 N` over `3 m`. Calculate the work if the region is triangular. Explain what each unit in `N m` represents and why it is equivalent to joules.

## Level 5: intentional error

A learner sees a constant velocity-time graph `v = 5 m/s` for `4 s` and writes: "the slope is zero, therefore displacement is zero". Locate the error, explain which question slope answers, and correct the displacement calculation.

Another learner calculates a negative area under a force-time curve and removes the sign because "area is always positive". Correct the reasoning: distinguish geometric area from signed area, state what the sign would mean for impulse, and write a complete physical interpretation.
