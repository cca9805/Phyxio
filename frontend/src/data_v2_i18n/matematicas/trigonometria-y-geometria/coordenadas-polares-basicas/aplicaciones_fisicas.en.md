# Physical applications

## 1. Kinematics

Physical context: motion with position, velocity, or trajectory. Operational use: calculate displacement or velocity components, estimate scale, and compare whether the sign matches the chosen axis. In this context, basic polar coordinates connects the drawing with a measurable quantity and avoids using a formula without geometry.

The important transfer is that unit, sign, scale, and model must survive the calculation. If the component, distance, angle, or ratio obtained contradicts the physical system, the angular reference, quadrant, and chosen relation are reviewed before trusting the number.
