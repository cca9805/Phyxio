# Practice

## Level 1: recognition

Identify in five drawings which objects represent vectors, scalars, components, and resultants. Write magnitude, direction, and sense when possible. If reference is missing, state which datum you would request before operating.

## Level 2: direct application

Calculate components of a vector (3, 4), its magnitude, and its approximate direction. Then add (3, 4) to (-1, 2) and write the result with symbolic unit.

## Level 3: choosing the tool

Decide whether each question asks for addition, subtraction, dot product, cross product, change of basis, or field reading: work of a force, torque, net force, relative velocity, and electric field at a point. Calculate at least one simple example and justify each choice carefully.

## Level 4: physical interpretation

Interpret a resultant negative in x and positive in y. Explain its sense and what cannot be concluded from magnitude alone. Connect the answer with force, velocity, or field.

## Level 5: intentional error

A learner adds 5 N to the right and 5 N upward and answers 10 N. Locate the error, explain why magnitudes are not added directly, and correct with components, magnitude, and direction.
