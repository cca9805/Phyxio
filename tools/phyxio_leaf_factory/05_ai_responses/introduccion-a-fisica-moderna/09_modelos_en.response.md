# Introduction to Modern Physics
## Ideal model
The ideal model treats energy exchange as quantized when the scale of the phenomenon requires it. Radiation is read through [[frecuencia]], [[longitud_onda]], and [[energia_cuantizada]], while bound systems are read through [[numero_cuantico]], [[energia_nivel]], and [[separacion_energia]]. Classical physics appears as an approximation when the discrete scale is hidden compared with [[escala_energia_clasica]].

## Hypotheses
- An identifiable quantum scale exists; otherwise [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] does not measure anything useful.
- Radiation is in a regime where [[velocidad_luz]], [[frecuencia]], and [[longitud_onda]] are compatible; if not, the photon-wave relation loses validity.
- The discrete levels represented by [[numero_cuantico]] are adequate; if the real system has richer structure, [[energia_nivel]] is only an approximation.
- Units remain coherent; mixing eV and J without conversion corrupts the physical result.

## Quantitative validity domain
The quantum model is necessary when [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] >= 10^-2 or when [[separacion_energia]] is comparable with the available energy. A classical approximation is usually reasonable if [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] <= 10^-6. For electromagnetic radiation in vacuum, [[frecuencia]] > 0 and [[longitud_onda]] > 0. In ideal level models, [[numero_cuantico]] must be a positive integer.

## Model failure signals
The simple model fails when a predicted transition does not occur even though total energy seems sufficient, when an experimental threshold appears, when emission or absorption occurs in discrete lines, or when a classical prediction allows values the system never accepts. It also fails if [[energia_cuantizada]] is treated as the total energy of a beam without considering photon number.

## Extended or alternative model
An extended model is needed for many coupled levels, spectral width, field interaction, relativity, or band structure. In real materials, [[energia_nivel]] may become energy bands and [[separacion_energia]] may become a gap with width and occupation conditions.

## Operational comparison
| Situation | Useful model | Criterion |
|---|---|---|
| Macroscopic wave | Classical | Very small [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] |
| Single photon | Quantum | Detectable [[energia_cuantizada]] |
| Isolated atom | Discrete levels | Relevant [[numero_cuantico]] |
| Real solid | Extended model | bands or many interactions |
