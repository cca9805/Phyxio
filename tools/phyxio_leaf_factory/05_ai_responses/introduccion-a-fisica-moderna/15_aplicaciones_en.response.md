# Applications
## 1. Image sensors
Dominant variable: [[energia_cuantizada]]
Validity limit: 10^14 Hz <= [[frecuencia]] <= 10^15 Hz
A digital sensor does not directly count a smooth classical wave; it records microscopic events in pixels. Each event depends on whether the [[energia_cuantizada]] of a photon can excite charge in the material. The measured output is electrical, but the prediction uses [[frecuencia]], [[longitud_onda]], and [[separacion_energia]]. Classical physics describes propagation to the lens; modern physics explains why some colors activate a material better than others.

## 2. Solar cells
Dominant variable: [[separacion_energia]]
Validity limit: [[energia_cuantizada]] >= [[separacion_energia]]
In a photovoltaic cell, the question is not only how much light arrives, but whether each photon exceeds the energy gap of the material. [[frecuencia]] controls energy per photon, and [[longitud_onda]] indicates the useful spectral region. The model predicts which radiation creates useful carriers and which part is lost as transmission or heat. The limit appears when a simple photon model ignores full band structure.

## 3. Atomic spectra
Dominant variable: [[energia_nivel]]
Validity limit: [[numero_cuantico]] positive integer
Spectra contain lines because atoms do not accept arbitrary energy. [[energia_nivel]] depends on the allowed state, and transitions are read through [[separacion_energia]]. One measures emitted or absorbed [[longitud_onda]] and infers the discrete structure of the system. This application constrains classical models: a continuous orbit cannot explain narrow lines without quantization.

## 4. Lasers
Dominant variable: [[frecuencia]]
Validity limit: spectral width much smaller than [[frecuencia]]
A laser combines a highly ordered wave reading with a quantum reading of stimulated emission. [[frecuencia]] fixes the [[energia_cuantizada]] of emitted photons, and coherence enables intense directional radiation. The model predicts color, energy per photon, and possible interaction with materials. The basic model fails if level populations, pumping, and losses are ignored.

## 5. Classical limit
Dominant variable: [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]]
Validity limit: [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] <= 10^-6
In macroscopic systems, individual quanta are often invisible because [[escala_energia_clasica]] is huge compared with [[separacion_energia]]. This application measures when a continuous model is acceptable. [[cociente_cuantico_clasico]] predicts whether discrete jumps matter. The validity limit does not mean quanta disappear; it means they fall below the relevant resolution.
