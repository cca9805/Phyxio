# Irreversibility and the Arrow of Time
## Conceptual context
Everyday experience separates past from future: a broken glass does not reassemble itself, a hot cup cools, and ink spreads in water. Microscopic laws can be nearly reversible, yet real macroscopic processes show a direction. That direction is understood through [[entropia]], [[numero_microestados]], and [[probabilidad_macroestado]]. The arrow of time is based on the statistical preference for macrostates with many compatible microstates.

## 🟢 Essential level
The arrow of time appears because real processes tend toward more probable macrostates. Looking only at the system can mislead: [[variacion_entropia_sistema]] may increase or decrease depending on how the boundary is chosen. The second-law reading must include the surroundings through [[variacion_entropia_universo]]. When a real process is irreversible, [[entropia_producida]] marks a physical direction: spontaneous evolution moves toward non-decreasing total entropy. [[tiempo]] orders the states, but it does not by itself explain why one direction is preferred. The explanation is statistical. A macrostate with larger [[numero_microestados]] has larger [[probabilidad_macroestado]].

## 🔴 Structural level
The model requires a clear system boundary. [[variacion_entropia_sistema]] can be negative without violating the second law if [[variacion_entropia_entorno]] compensates it. Therefore the relevant invariant for an isolated spontaneous evolution is [[variacion_entropia_universo]]. The model assumes well-defined macroscopic states, a meaningful entropy balance, and a time scale over which equilibrium or local equilibrium can be discussed. It also assumes that irreversibility is captured by [[entropia_producida]]. In the reversible ideal limit, generated entropy vanishes. In real processes such as friction, diffusion, free expansion, mixing, and heat transfer across a finite temperature difference, generated entropy is positive. The statistical link comes from [[numero_microestados]], [[probabilidad_macroestado]], and [[constante_boltzmann]]. The model fails if entropy is treated as visual messiness, if the surroundings are ignored, if units are mixed, or if microscopic fluctuations are used as stable macroscopic reversals. The Coord graph supports the structural reading: [[tiempo]] orders states, while [[variacion_entropia_universo]] shows whether the evolution has a physical arrow.

## Deep physical interpretation
[[entropia]] measures how many microscopic arrangements are compatible with a macroscopic description. A real irreversible process carries the total system toward regions with far greater statistical weight. [[entropia_producida]] signals that initial constraints have been dispersed. The arrow of time emerges because high-multiplicity macrostates are overwhelmingly more probable than finely prepared states.

## Order of magnitude
Laboratory entropy changes may be J/K or mJ/K, while microscopic changes are compared with [[constante_boltzmann]]. If [[variacion_entropia_universo]] exceeds a few units of [[constante_boltzmann]] by a large factor, spontaneous return is extremely unlikely.

## Special cases and extended example
A local entropy decrease can occur if the surroundings increase more. Free expansion increases accessible microstates. A reversible process is an ideal limit with no generated entropy. A drop of ink spreading in water is the classic example: microscopic motion may be reversible, but the mixed macrostate has vastly more compatible microstates than the separated one.
