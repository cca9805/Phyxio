# Irreversibility and the Arrow of Time
## Ideal model
The ideal model treats system and surroundings as clearly defined. The physical direction is decided by [[variacion_entropia_universo]], not by visual appearance. If real irreversibility is present, [[entropia_producida]] appears and the process departs from the reversible limit.

## Hypotheses
- The system-surroundings boundary is defined; if it changes, [[variacion_entropia_sistema]] and [[variacion_entropia_entorno]] cannot be compared directly.
- Macroscopic states have defined [[entropia]]; without local equilibrium, the balance is approximate.
- Macrostate statistics are relevant; if [[numero_microestados]] does not represent accessible configurations, the probability reading fails.
- Entropy units are coherent; mixing J/K with multiples of [[constante_boltzmann]] without conversion distorts the result.

## Quantitative validity domain
For isolated spontaneous processes, [[variacion_entropia_universo]] >= 0. The reversible ideal is approached when [[entropia_producida]] <= 10^-6 J/K for a reference macroscopic process or when it is negligible relative to experimental resolution. For microscopic systems, comparison with [[constante_boltzmann]] is more appropriate.

## Model failure signals
The simple model fails if a sustained negative trend in [[variacion_entropia_universo]] is claimed, if the surroundings are omitted, if a local fluctuation is read as a global reversal, or if the process is so far from equilibrium that state entropy is not meaningful.

## Extended or alternative model
An extended model is needed for non-equilibrium systems, mesoscopic fluctuations, information dynamics, or spatially complex transport. Local entropy production, transport equations, or stochastic thermodynamics may then be required.
