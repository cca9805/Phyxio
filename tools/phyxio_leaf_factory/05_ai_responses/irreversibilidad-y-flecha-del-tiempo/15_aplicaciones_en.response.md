# Applications
## 1. Cooling everyday objects
Dominant variable: [[variacion_entropia_universo]]
Validity limit: [[variacion_entropia_universo]] >= 0
When a hot cup cools, temperature is measured, but the time direction is interpreted through total entropy. The cup may lose internal energy while the air gains entropy. The application predicts which direction occurs spontaneously and constrains interpretations that look only at the visible object.

## 2. Diffusion and mixing
Dominant variable: [[numero_microestados]]
Validity limit: [[numero_microestados]] > 1
Perfume spreading in a room shows how mixed macrostates have vastly more compatible configurations. Concentration is measured, but the direction is explained with [[probabilidad_macroestado]] and [[entropia]]. Reverse unmixing is not logically impossible; it is physically unobservable because of probability.

## 3. Mechanical friction
Dominant variable: [[entropia_producida]]
Validity limit: [[entropia_producida]] > 0
A sliding block that stops disperses mechanical energy thermally. The relevant quantity is [[entropia_producida]], which measures departure from the reversible limit. The application predicts that motion will not spontaneously recover without changing the surroundings.

## 4. Real heat engines
Dominant variable: [[grado_irreversibilidad]]
Validity limit: [[grado_irreversibilidad]] >= 0
A real engine never operates with perfect reversibility. Friction, finite temperature differences, and losses generate entropy. [[grado_irreversibilidad]] helps compare designs and detect departure from the ideal. Possible efficiency is constrained by the second law.

## 5. Microscopic fluctuations
Dominant variable: [[constante_boltzmann]]
Validity limit: scale comparable with multiples of [[constante_boltzmann]]
Small systems can show fluctuations that seem to reverse a local tendency. This application requires comparing changes with [[constante_boltzmann]]. At macroscopic scale, such fluctuations do not invalidate the time arrow; they show the statistical root of the second law.
