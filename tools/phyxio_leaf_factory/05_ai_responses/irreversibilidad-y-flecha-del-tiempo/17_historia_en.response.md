# Irreversibility and the Arrow of Time
## Historical problem
The historical problem appeared when nearly reversible mechanical laws were compared with clearly irreversible everyday phenomena. Microscopic equations allowed velocities to be reversed, but heat flowed from hot to cold, gases mixed, and real engines lost ability to produce work.

## Prior conceptual difficulty
The difficulty was explaining why a time direction appears if individual particles do not seem to know past and future. A visual disorder explanation was not enough. Physics needed a connection between [[entropia]], counting possibilities, [[numero_microestados]], and [[probabilidad_macroestado]].

## What changed
Boltzmann introduced a statistical reading: the most probable macrostates are those with the most compatible microstates. With [[constante_boltzmann]], macroscopic entropy became linked to the microscopic world. Irreversibility stopped being a mysterious force and became an overwhelming statistical tendency.

## Impact on physics
This changed the meaning of the second law. [[variacion_entropia_universo]] became a criterion for physical direction, while [[entropia_producida]] distinguished real processes from ideal reversible limits.

## Connection with modern physics
The arrow of time remains connected with information, cosmology, fluctuations, and stochastic thermodynamics. This leaf summarizes that inheritance: the physical future is the side toward which the most probable macrostates point.
